the team

Alex Gil is Senior Lecturer II and Associate Research Faculty of Digital Humanities in the Department of Spanish and Portuguese at Yale University. He is currently senior editor of archipelagos journal. He has been a prolific producer and contributing team member of many recognized digital humanities projects and scholarly software including, Torn Apart/Separados, In The Same Boats, and more recently, (Un)Silencing Slavery.

Alexandre White, Co-PI, Black Beyond Data, PI, Underwriting Souls Assistant Professor, Department of Sociology and the Department of the History of Medicine, Johns Hopkins University and School of Medicine Alexandre White is the PI of Underwriting Souls, a project to digitize the archives of Lloyd’s, the insurance market pertaining to  trans-Atlantic slavery. This work produced a digital archive and online exhibitions based on these materials as well as a partnership which produced a visual art and poetry volume.  In addition to his role as faculty he is also the Associate Director for the Center for Medical Humanities and Social Medicine. His research foci, at the intersections of the sociology of race and ethnicity, history of medicine, and global historical sociology, critically investigates global racial formations and how racial forms of governance, rooted primarily in imperial projects, have produced contemporary social phenomena. White’s first book Epidemic Orientalism was published by Stanford University Press. This book explores how epidemic threats become the focus of international management, regulation and control, as well as the political, economic and racial ideologies that have shaped international coordination to stop pandemic spread.
