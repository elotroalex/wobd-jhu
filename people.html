The Organizers

Jessica Marie Johnson is an Associate Professor in the Department of History at the Johns Hopkins University. As a historian and Black Studies scholar, Johnson researches black diasporic freedom struggles from slavery to emancipation. As a digital humanist, Johnson explores ways digital and social media disseminate and create historical narratives, in particular, comparative histories of slavery and people of African descent. She is the Director of LifexCode: Digital Humanities Against Enclosure. She is also a PI on Black Beyond Data, a Black Studies computational humanities lab initiative with Kim Gallon Black Press Research Collective and Alexandre White. She is co-PI on the Diaspora Solidarities Lab a virtual Black feminist pedagogies and practice lab.

Alex Gil is Senior Lecturer II and Associate Research Faculty of Digital Humanities in the Department of Spanish and Portuguese at Yale University. He is currently senior editor of archipelagos journal. He has been a prolific producer and contributing team member of many recognized digital humanities projects and scholarly software including, Torn Apart/Separados, In The Same Boats, and more recently, (Un)Silencing Slavery.

Alexandre White is an Assistant Professor of Sociology and of the History of Medicine at Johns Hopkins University and School of Medicine. He is the PI of Underwriting Souls, a project to digitize the archives of Lloyd’s, the insurance market pertaining to  trans-Atlantic slavery. This work produced a digital archive and online exhibitions based on these materials as well as a partnership which produced a visual art and poetry volume.  In addition to his role as faculty, he is also the Associate Director for the Center for Medical Humanities and Social Medicine. His research foci, at the intersections of the sociology of race and ethnicity, history of medicine, and global historical sociology, critically investigates global racial formations and how racial forms of governance, rooted primarily in imperial projects, have produced contemporary social phenomena. White’s first book Epidemic Orientalism was published by Stanford University Press. This book explores how epidemic threats become the focus of international management, regulation and control, as well as the political, economic and racial ideologies that have shaped international coordination to stop pandemic spread.
